# Linux - Processes - Signals


• Signals are software interrupts
• Signals notify a process that something has happened
◇ If hardware issues occur, the kernel notifies the process
◇ If hardware issues occur, the kernel notifies the process

## Signals & processes


• Delivery
◇ When a signal is generated by some event, it gets delivered to a process
◇ The signal is in a pending state until it's delivered
• Processes have signal masks
◇ Signal masks can be set to block signal delivery
◇ Some signals are unblockable, like SIGKILL. The KILL signal destroys the process.
• When a signal is delivered, a process can do a multitude of things:
◇ Ignore the signal
◇ "Catch" the signal and perform a specific handler routine
◇ The process can be terminated, as opposed to the normal exit system call
◇ Block the signal, depending on the signal mask

## Common signals


• Each signal is defined by integers with symbolic names
• They're in the form of SIGxxx
• Common signals include:
◇ SIGHUP or HUP or 1: Hangup
◇ SIGINT or INT or 2: Interrupt (Ctrl-C)
◇ SIGKILL or KILL or 9: Kill
◇ SIGSEGV or SEGV or 11: Segmentation fault
◇ SIGTERM or TERM or 15: Software termination
◇ SIGSTOP or STOP: Stop

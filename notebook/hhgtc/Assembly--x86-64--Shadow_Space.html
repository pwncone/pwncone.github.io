# x64 Shadow Space


Also called Spill Space and Home Space

https://stackoverflow.com/questions/30190132/what-is-the-shadow-space-in-x64-assembly
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shadow_stack

The shadow space exists to make x64 debugging easier.
In x64, the first 4 arguments of a function are passed into registers.
Therfore, when debugging your code, you won't be able to check the first 4 of your function arguments after you've stepped through the code.

This where the shadow space is used.
The compiler leaves a copy of the register values in the shadow space so that they're later visible when debugging.

The shadow space always 32bits in size,
and is reserved before a function call.

# Linux - Commands - Disk/filesystem management



## Disk Usage


### df


df shows you the utilization of your currently mounted filesystems.
You can see what the device is, and how much capacity is used and available.

• -h = human readable format
pete@icebox:~$ df -h
Filesystem     1K-blocks    Used Available Use% Mounted on
/dev/sda1       6.2G  2.3G  3.6G  40% /


### du


du shows you the disk usage of the current directory you are in.
du is useful if you want to find files to delete to free up disk space on your drive.

du -h


## Filesystem Repair


• fsck (filesystem check) is used to check the consistency of a filesystem
• When you boot up a disk, fsck will run before your disk is mounted to make sure everything is ok.
• Sometimes your disk will be so bad that you'll need to manually do this
◇ HOWEVER, you should do this while your filesystem isn't mounted
◇ i.e. from a rescure disk/somewhere where you can access your filesystem without it being mounted

sudo fsck /dev/sda

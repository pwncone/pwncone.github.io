Bitshifts are how you shift bits in a number.
>> shift bits to the right
<< shift bits to the left

Here's a value:
0x12345678

Here's what it looks like in binary:
00010010 00110100 01010110 01111000
0x12 0x34 0x56 0x78

The low order byte in 0x12345678 is 0x78
The high order byte is 0x12



By shifting 24 bits to the right, you can retrieve the high order byte - 0x12.
By shifting 16 bits to the right, you can retrieve the 2 high order bytes 0x1234.




#include <stdio.h>

int main()
{
int val1 = 0x12345678;
printf("val 1: 0x%x \n", val1);
printf("\t 2 lower bytes: 0x%x \n", val1 & 0x0000ffff);
printf("\t 2 higher bytes: 0x%x \n", (val1 >> 16) & 0x0000ffff);

int val2 = 0xABCD;
printf("val 2: 0x%x \n", val2);
printf("\t lower byte: 0x%x \n", val2 & 0x00ff);
printf("\t higher byte: 0x%x \n", (val2 >> 8) & 0x00ff);

return 0;
}
